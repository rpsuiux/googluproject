Prototype validation

Ideate and Create Prototypes

  • After you've organize your research, discuss the findings. What do you see? Focus discussions around common patterns, pain points and outliers. These will be where insights typically exist. Document these insights, providing support for why you think this. Creating the right solution to the right problem.In context of the project or problem you are trying to solve, prioritize and rank these. What is the most valuable to solve?
  • Using the insights, brainstorm or ideate possible solutions. There are many creative activities to generate ideas, so pick the best that works for your team. The goal here is to create ideas in the context for the insights.Design Thinking is an extremely helpful approach to center your team around creating empathic solutions to any problem.So, as an example, if a consumer has a pain point at a specific part of your product journey, what solutions could you create to solve it? Cluster all of the solutions and discuss as a team.
  • When you are ready, empower the team to create quick prototypes, models, diagrams or drawings of the top ideas. Depending on your resources, there are many ways for you to get creative in this step, using found materials or setting creative limitations on budget. These can be shared with end-users and consumers for quick feedback and idea builds.
  •  Test and Summarize Learnings: The last phase of Design Thinking is to evaluate, test and refine. In order to complete the process, you’ll summarize your findings by laying out the different solutions based on your research. From here, certain teams or managers may further refine ideas or even take the final step to selecting an actual idea with which to move forward. No matter what, it's imperative to collaborate openly with consumers and end-users. They have the pain, so it’s important to make sure that your solution is solving an actual need.

Innovation Implementation
  • Customer Validation Let customers experience the innovation and learn.
  • Testing for desirability: I test the innovation concepts for desirability, whether customers want to use your service or product. This is an essential step to ensure that you are addressing the right problem. Let customers experience the innovation and give feedback. 8 – 10 people bring out 80% of the problems and needs. Test towards a minimal viable product. From talking to our customers we immediately saw that we had an inside-out view. The design thinking process helped us to turn this around.
